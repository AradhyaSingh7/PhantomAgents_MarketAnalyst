##Product Development and Demonstrations##
On August 1, 2020, the company (C019) demonstrated a high-reflectivity heliostat array in Arizona. This product demonstration generated early customer traction.

##Market Expansion and Revenue Generation##
The company secured its first European pilot Concentrated Solar Power (CSP) project with a Spanish Utility on October 12, 2021. This contract win resulted in a $15 million Engineering, Procurement, and Construction (EPC) contract. On November 20, 2022, the company launched its HelioStore, a thermal battery enabling nighttime power, which led to market expansion.

##Strategic Partnerships and New Markets##
On July 7, 2023, a joint development agreement was signed with ACWA Power for CSP projects in the Middle East and North Africa (MENA) region, marking a new region entry. Further solidifying its strategic presence, a joint venture was formed on February 10, 2025, with ACWA Power and EDF for utility-scale solar projects, expanding the company's EPC portfolio.

##Operational Challenges and Resolutions##
The company faced an operational challenge reported on February 18, 2024, where water scarcity in the MENA region caused delays in cooling at a CSP plant, leading to a temporary Power Purchase Agreement (PPA) delay. This issue was resolved on September 9, 2024, with the implementation of a dry-cooling redesign, normalizing operations.

##Financial Performance##
On August 19, 2025, the company released its Q2 results, which exceeded expectations, recording the highest ever EPC contract bookings and leading to stock gains.
